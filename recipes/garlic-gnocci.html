Creamy Garlic Mushroom Gnocchi


Here's a step by step guide on how to make the perfect garlic mushroom gnocci.

What is it?

While the gnocchi is cooking, you'll caramelise mushrooms with roasted garlic, before stirring them into a creamy mustard sauce. Top with Italian hard cheese and freshly torn basil, and dig in!

Takes 10 minutes.

What do I need?

  • Spinach (80g)
  • Crème fraîche (80g)
  • Dijon mustard (5.5g)
  • Grated Italian hard cheese (30g)
  • Roasted garlic paste (15g)
  • Basil (5g)
  • Gnocchi (350g)
  • Chestnut mushrooms (160g)
  • Soy sauce (15ml)

You'll also need

olive oil, pepper (or dietary alternatives)


How do I make it?

  1. Boil a full kettle
  2. Heat a large, wide-based pan (preferably non-stick) with a drizzle of olive oil over a medium-high heat
  3. Tear your chestnut mushrooms into rough bite-sized pieces
  4. Once hot, add the torn mushrooms and your roasted garlic paste to the pan and cook for 2-3 min or until starting to caramelise
  5. Add your gnocchi to a pot of boiled water and bring to the boil over a high heat
  6. Cook the gnocchi for 3 min or until it begins to rise to the top of the pot
  7. Once done, drain the gnocchi, reserving a cup of the starchy cooking water
  8. Wash your spinach, then pat it dry with kitchen paper
  9. Once the mushrooms have started to caramelise, add the spinach and your soy sauce to the pan and cook for 2-3 min or until starting to wilt
  10. Once done, add your crème fraîche and Dijon mustard and stir everything together
  11. Add the drained gnocchi and half of your grated Italian hard cheese (save the rest for garnish!) and give everything a good mix up – this is your creamy garlic mushroom gnocchi
  12. Tip: Add a splash of the reserved starchy cooking water if it's looking a little dry
  13. Serve the creamy garlic mushroom gnocchi
  14. Tear your basil leaves over the top
  15. Garnish with the remaining grated Italian hard cheese and a generous grind of black pepper
  16. Enjoy!
