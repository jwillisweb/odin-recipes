Truffled Mushroom Risotto with Hazelnuts & Cheese Tuiles


Here's a step by step guide on how to make the perfect truffled mushroom risotto.

What is it?

Thyme-roasted flat white mushrooms top a bed of caramelised leek and chestnut mushroom risotto, garnished with homemade cheese tuiles, rich truffle oil and toasted hazelnuts. Delicious seasonal decadence!

Takes 40 minutes.

What do I need?

  • Vegetable stock mix (11g)
  • Hazelnuts (25g)
  • Italian hard cheese (20g)
  • Garlic clove x2
  • Chestnut mushrooms (160g)
  • Thyme (5g)
  • Chinese rice wine (30ml)
  • Leek
  • Truffle flavoured extra virgin olive oil (16ml)
  • Flat white mushrooms (150g)
  • Arborio rice (160g)

You'll also need

butter, olive oil, pepper, salt, water (or dietary alternatives)


How do I make it?

  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C/ 180°C (fan)/ gas 6 and boil a kettle
  2. Chop your chestnut mushrooms finely
  3. Peel and finely chop (or grate) your garlic
  4. Chop your leek in half lengthways, wash thoroughly to remove any grit then top, tail and slice finely
  5. Crush your hazelnuts roughly in the bag with a rolling pin
  6. Heat a large, dry, wide-based pan (preferably non-stick) over a medium heat, then once hot, add the crushed hazelnuts and cook for 1-2 min or until lightly coloured, then transfer to a plate and set aside, reserve the pan
  7. Tip: Watch them like a hawk to make sure they don't burn!
  8. Dissolve your vegetable stock mix in 750ml boiled water
  9. Grate your Italian hard cheese
  10. Return the pan to a medium heat with a generous drizzle of olive oil, add the sliced leek with a generous pinch of salt and cook for 8-10 min or until softened, then add the chopped garlic and cook for 2-3 min further
  11. Meanwhile, strip your thyme leaves from their stems, discard the stems
  12. Add your Chinese rice wine with half the thyme leaves (save the rest for later!) and the chopped mushrooms and cook for 3-4 min or until starting to soften
  13. Once the mushrooms have started to soften, add your arborio rice and cook for 1 min or until the grains are fully coated in the oil
  14. Add the vegetable stock a ladle at a time, stirring continuously for 25-30 min or until all of the stock is absorbed and the rice is cooked
  15. Line a baking tray with non-stick baking paper, then slice your flat white mushroom[s] into wedges and add to the tray
  16. Sprinkle over the remaining thyme leaves, drizzle with olive oil and season generously with salt and black pepper
  17. Put the tray in the oven and cook for 5-6 min or until the flat white mushroom wedges are cooked through – these are your thyme-roasted flat white mushrooms
  18. Line a separate baking tray with non-stick baking paper, add 1/4 of the grated Italian hard cheese (you'll use the rest later!) to the tray, and form into a long rectangular shape
  19. Put the tray in the oven for 7-8 min or until the cheese has completely melted and is starting to brown
  20. Once melted, remove the tray from the oven and set aside to cool
  21. Once cooled, snap into 1 equal sized piece per person – these are your cheese tuiles
  22. Once the rice is cooked, remove the pan from the heat
  23. Stir in half your truffle oil (save the rest for later!), the remaining grated Italian hard cheese and a large knob of butter – this is your truffled mushroom risotto
  24. Serve the truffled mushroom risotto topped with the thyme-roasted flat white mushrooms and crushed hazelnuts
  25. Drizzle with the remaining truffle oil and top with a cheese tuile
  26. Enjoy!
